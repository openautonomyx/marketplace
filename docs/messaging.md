# Messaging & Marketing Communication

The canonical positioning and marketing copy for OpenAutonomyX Marketplace. All
external communication ladders up to one thesis:

> Popularity is a vanity metric. Trust is contextual.

> For enterprises adopting AI and MCP skills, **OpenAutonomyX** is the certified

## Elevator pitch (30s)

Public skill directories rank by downloads. That tells an enterprise nothing about
whether a skill is safe for its regulated data, compatible with its toolchain, or
reliable for its workflow. OpenAutonomyX evaluates, benchmarks, certifies, and
signs every skill **against your actual context** — then registers it so your teams
can find skills proven to perform *here*. Popularity is a vanity metric. Trust is
contextual.

## Voice & usage notes

- Lead with the contrast (popularity vs. performance-in-context); it is the wedge.
- Never claim a universal score. Always tie trust to a context and scope.
- Be precise about signing: contracts are digest-signed today; cryptographic
  (DID/VC, Sigstore) signing is a stated roadmap item. Do not overstate it.
